## Recommended Next Steps

The book is unusually reliable for its genre. Across 43 files and 207 flagged assertions, the fact-check pass found **zero outdated and zero contradicted claims** — every named study (VanLehn 2011, Roediger & Karpicke 2006, Kluger & DeNisi, Wisniewski et al., Dunlosky et al. 2013, Schroeder 2018, Graham & Perin, Coleman 1966, Scarfe 2024, Bastani et al. 2025) verified to its correct title, journal, and figures, and every Hattie effect size matched the 2018 252-influence list the book explicitly anchors to. EVIDENCE was by far the largest category (139 of 207), reflecting a book built on attributed empirical findings rather than recommendations — notably, **no GUIDELINE-type standard-of-care claims and only one APPROVAL claim** appeared, which removes the highest-risk category entirely.

The most urgent items for expert review are not errors but **source-attachment gaps**: the five labor-market statistics in Chapter 97 (GPA-screening decline, skills-based-hiring share, degree-requirement drops, AI-skill wage premiums, the 88% would-upskill figure) are directionally consistent with public data but are not yet tied to named sources — these should have citations attached before publication, as unsourced specific percentages are the book's likeliest point of future challenge. Second, three editorial consistency notes recur and are worth a single cleanup pass: (1) the **Bastani et al. (2025)** paper should be cited by its full published title, "Generative AI *without guardrails* can harm learning: Evidence from high school mathematics" — dropping "without guardrails" inverts the paper's actual finding (a linter has already restored this in most chapters); (2) **Bergeron & Rivard (2017)** is more precisely cited as Bergeron (author), Rivard (translator); and (3) the **self-reported grades** effect size appears as d = 1.33 in Chapters 5 and 93 but is commonly listed as 1.44 — an edition difference worth standardizing to the book's stated 2018 baseline. The five priority UNVERIFIED findings (Fendick 1990 r ≈ .35; the Kosmyna EEG preprint; the "no LLM decodable-text evaluation exists" negative claim; two grouped effect-size averages pending MetaX re-derivation) are all already hedged in the prose with the author's own `[verify]` tags and represent low residual risk. Overall reliability picture: **high** — the manuscript's heavy, disciplined self-hedging (`[verify]`, `[contested]`, `[HYPOTHESIS]` tags) did most of the protective work, and the verification pass largely resolved those flags favorably rather than uncovering problems.
