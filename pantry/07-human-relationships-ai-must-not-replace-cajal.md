## Figure 2 — AI on the perimeter, human at the event  ·  Important
**Heuristic:** VG
**Concept (one sentence):** AI's legitimate role sits before and after the human relational event — translating, freeing time, detecting gaps, preparing, documenting — but never constitutes the event itself.
**Figure type:** systems diagram (before/after perimeter around a protected core)

**Block 1 — Illustrae paste block:** Create a single-column perimeter diagram, 89mm wide at 300 DPI, vector, white background, organized as a left-to-right flow with a protected central event. Place a central node representing the human relational event (the conference, the repair conversation, the real contact), enclosed in a boundary ring. To its left, place a cluster of "before" AI-support nodes (translate, free relational time, detect gaps, prepare/rehearse) with single-headed arrows pointing toward the event but stopping at the boundary ring. To its right, place a cluster of "after" AI-support nodes (document, answer transactional follow-ups) with single-headed arrows leading away from the event, again originating at but not penetrating the boundary. The central event node is reached and left only by human presence, depicted as a distinct uninterrupted band passing through the core. Color the AI before/after nodes orange, the protected human-event core blue, and the boundary ring plus the human band bluish green. Flat nodes, uniform thin strokes, single-headed arrows. Request an unannotated vector diagram with no baked-in text.
