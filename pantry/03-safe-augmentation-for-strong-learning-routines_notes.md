## A. Conceptual foundations

### Augmentation preserves the mechanism
This chapter sits in an AI-augmented zone. The examples (34. Field independence (d=0.68), 35. Acceleration programs (d=0.68), 45. Relations of high school to university achievement (d=0.6)) can benefit from faster planning, better examples, richer diagnosis, more varied practice, or clearer feedback, but the teacher and learner still carry the decisive cognitive and pedagogical work.

The author should define augmentation as support around the mechanism, not replacement of the mechanism. AI helps prepare, vary, monitor, and summarize; humans still decide, teach, attempt, discuss, and revise.

**Common misconception:** Readers may treat any time saving as improvement. Time saving is only improvement if the saved time returns to learning.

**Worked example:** AI drafts three worked-example variants; the teacher chooses one based on misconceptions seen yesterday; students solve a transfer problem without AI.

**Source(s):** U.S. Department of Education, Artificial Intelligence and Future of Teaching and Learning (2023): https://www.ed.gov/sites/ed/files/documents/ai-report/ai-report.pdf; Visible Learning MetaX research methodology and d=0.40 hinge: https://www.visiblelearningmetax.com/research_methodology

### Teacher judgment remains load-bearing
Many augmented influences depend on sequencing, calibration, clarity, diagnosis, and responsiveness. AI can propose, but the teacher must decide whether the proposal fits learners, prior knowledge, goals, and evidence.

The chapter should make teacher judgment visible rather than assuming it. A good case study shows the point where AI suggests something plausible and the teacher rejects or revises it.

**Common misconception:** A plausible AI plan is mistaken for a pedagogically valid plan.

**Worked example:** AI suggests acceleration for a student; the teacher checks prerequisite evidence, motivation, family context, and support before making a placement decision.

**Source(s):** OECD, AI and the Future of Skills, Volume 2 (2023): https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/ai-and-the-future-of-skills-volume-2_a9fe53cb-en.html; U.S. Department of Education, Artificial Intelligence and Future of Teaching and Learning (2023): https://www.ed.gov/sites/ed/files/documents/ai-report/ai-report.pdf

### Evidence beyond artifact quality
Augmented workflows often improve artifacts: clearer plans, better rubrics, nicer examples, faster comments. The evidence must be learning evidence: delayed performance, transfer, explanation quality, teacher action, or improved intervention response.

The chapter should build the habit of separating output polish from capability growth.

**Common misconception:** Teams celebrate smoother workflow and forget to measure student learning.

**Worked example:** AI-generated formative items look good; the evidence is whether they reveal misconceptions and lead to improved performance after reteaching.

**Source(s):** Hattie & Timperley, The Power of Feedback (2007): https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.3102/003465430298487; Roediger & Karpicke, Test-enhanced learning / retrieval practice (2006): https://www.psychologicalscience.org/journals/psychological-science/j.1467-9280.2006.01693.x/

## B. Domain examples and cases

### Case 1: AI as preparation accelerator
A teacher uses AI to prepare examples, diagnostic prompts, or formative checks for 34. Field independence (d=0.68). The teacher chooses, edits, and sequences; students still attempt and explain.

### Case 2: Professional learning loop
A department uses AI to summarize student work patterns before a meeting. The human team interprets causes, chooses an intervention, and returns with evidence.

### Failure case: Outsourcing the hard part
The tool generates plans, feedback, or summaries so fluently that teachers or learners stop doing the diagnostic, reflective, or retrieval work that made the influence effective.

## D. Current state of the field

**Settled:**
- Effect sizes are useful prioritization signals, not local guarantees.
- AI adoption should be judged by the learning mechanism it preserves or disrupts.
- Delayed, unaided performance is stronger evidence of learning than in-tool fluency or artifact polish.

**Contested or emerging:**
- Hattie-style rankings are methodologically debated and should not be treated as exact prescriptions.
- Generative AI evidence in education is fast-moving; many claims are still vendor, observational, or early-stage rather than causal.
