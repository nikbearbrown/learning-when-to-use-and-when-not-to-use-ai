## Figure 3 — Human tutoring vs. ITS vs. AI-replaced peer tutoring  ·  Supplementary
**Heuristic:** PQ
**Concept (one sentence):** VanLehn found human tutoring (0.79) and step-based ITS (0.76) statistically comparable on the tutee side, yet the dataset hypothesizes peer tutoring drops to roughly 0.10 when the peer exchange is removed — because the tutee side is not where most of the effect lives.
**Figure type:** Horizontal dot plot against the 0.40 hinge line, value axis from zero.

**Block 1 — Illustrae paste block:** Create a horizontal dot plot on a white single-column canvas, 89mm wide, 300 DPI, flat vector. Plot four values along a value axis beginning at zero and running to about 0.85, ordered top to bottom by magnitude: human tutoring (0.79), step-based intelligent tutoring systems (0.76), peer tutoring as classified (0.53), and AI-replaced peer tutoring hypothesis (≈0.10). Draw one prominent vertical hinge line at 0.40. The 0.79 and 0.76 points should sit close together to show their statistical comparability; the ≈0.10 hypothesis point should sit well left of the hinge to show the collapse. Mark the ≈0.10 point as a hypothesis using a distinct hollow or secondary marker style. Use blue for the confirmed effect sizes, reddish purple for the hypothesis marker, neutral mid-gray for the hinge line, light gray for the axis. Proportional ink. Maximum six labeled components. One-pixel strokes, white background, unannotated vector.
