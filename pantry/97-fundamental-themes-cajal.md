## Figure 1 — The Irreducibly Human tier taxonomy (where machines win, where humans are irreplaceable)  ·  Critical
**Heuristic:** VG
**Concept (one sentence):** Human cognitive capacity is organized into tiers where AI is superhuman at the bottom (Tier 1: pattern and association) and structurally weak-to-absent at the top (Tiers 4–7: metacognition, causal reasoning, collective intelligence, wisdom), with a phase gate at the Tier 1 / Tier 4 boundary.
**Figure type:** Vertical hierarchy / stacked-tier schematic with a marked boundary line.

**Block 1 — Illustrae paste block:** Create a flat vector hierarchy schematic on a white single-column canvas, 89mm wide, 300 DPI, built as horizontal bands stacked vertically. From bottom to top place six tier bands representing, in order: Tier 1 (pattern and association), Tier 2 (embodied and sensorimotor), Tier 4 (metacognitive and supervisory), Tier 5 (causal and counterfactual), Tier 6 (collective and distributed), Tier 7 (existential and wisdom). The bottom band is where machines win; make it visually dominant and fully filled. The upper four bands are where humans are irreducible; render them as a connected group. Draw one prominent horizontal boundary line between the lower machine region and the upper human region, positioned just above Tier 2, representing the phase gate. Map the bottom machine-dominant tier to blue, the embodied tier to gray, and the four upper human tiers to bluish green with increasing emphasis toward the top. Make the boundary line the strongest single element. Keep to six bands plus one boundary line. Request an unannotated vector with no tier numbers and no text.

## Figure 2 — The friction findings: practice gain vs. unassisted-exam collapse  ·  Critical
**Heuristic:** PQ
**Concept (one sentence):** Free AI use during practice produced a large apparent gain (48% higher during practice) but a real loss on the unassisted exam (17 percentage points lower), and AI-assisted writing reduced functional brain connectivity by up to 55% — the fluency of output diverged sharply from the neural events of learning.
**Figure type:** Two-panel proportional bar chart — Panel A paired before/after-style bars (Bastani), Panel B a single magnitude bar (Kosmyna).

**Block 1 — Illustrae paste block:** Create a flat vector two-panel bar chart on a white single-column canvas, 89mm wide, 300 DPI. Panel A, on the left, shows two vertical bars on a shared zero-based axis: one positive bar of height 48 representing the practice-phase performance gain, and one negative bar extending below the zero baseline to -17 representing the percentage-point drop on the unassisted exam. The positive bar is the "appearance of learning"; the negative bar is the real outcome. Panel B, on the right, shows a single vertical bar of height 55 on its own zero-based axis representing the reduction in functional brain connectivity during AI-assisted writing. Anchor both panels at zero with proportional bar heights. Map the positive practice-gain bar to orange (apparent benefit), the negative exam-drop bar to vermillion (real harm), and the brain-connectivity reduction bar to vermillion as well. Keep bars uniform width. Request an unannotated vector with no numeric labels, no axis text, and no panel titles.

**Block 2 — Full SCOPE prompt:**
- **S:** Single-column 89mm, 300 DPI, vector, white background, two-panel layout.
- **C:** Panel A (Bastani): practice-phase gain +48% (positive bar), unassisted-exam change −17 percentage points (negative bar below baseline). Panel B (Kosmyna EEG): up to 55% reduction in functional brain connectivity, AI-assisted vs brain-only writing (single bar). All values confirmed in source.
- **O:** Two side-by-side panels, each with a zero baseline. Panel A: one upward bar (+48) and one downward bar (−17) sharing the baseline. Panel B: one upward magnitude bar (55). Proportional ink throughout.

## Figure 3 — The phase gate: Tier 1 delegation vs. Tier 4–7 protection  ·  Critical
**Heuristic:** MC
**Concept (one sentence):** The division of labor is operationalized as a phase gate where AI handles Tier 1 pattern work and the human performs the Tier 4–7 cognitive work, with the gate sitting exactly at the Tier 1 / Tier 4 boundary and AI blocked from crossing it.
**Figure type:** Horizontal two-zone systems diagram with a central gate (left-to-right flow).

**Block 1 — Illustrae paste block:** Create a flat vector systems diagram on a white single-column canvas, 89mm wide, 300 DPI, laid out horizontally left to right. On the left, draw an AI zone containing a small cluster of Tier 1 task nodes: generating structure, retrieving information, adjusting complexity, producing feedback, flagging errors, executing estimation. An arrow leads rightward from this zone toward a central vertical gate element. The gate permits the supporting flow through but blocks AI from performing the work on the right: draw a perpendicular blockage bar across the AI-to-human-work path at the gate. On the right, draw a human zone containing Tier 4–7 task nodes: plausibility auditing, problem formulation, causal identification, holding a position, institutional judgment, accountability. Keep each zone to six nodes maximum. Map the AI zone and its nodes to blue, the gate to gray, and the human zone and its nodes to bluish green. The blockage bar at the gate is vermillion. Use single-headed arrows only. Request an unannotated vector with no text on any node.

**Block 2 — Full SCOPE prompt:**
- **S:** Single-column 89mm, 300 DPI, vector, white background, horizontal L-to-R flow.
- **C:** Left AI zone — Tier 1 tasks (confirmed): generate structure, retrieve information, adjust complexity, produce calibrated feedback, flag errors in well-defined domains, execute estimation. Right human zone — Tiers 4–7 tasks (confirmed): plausibility auditing, problem formulation, causal identification layer, hold/defend a position, institutional judgment, accountability. Central phase gate at the Tier 1 / Tier 4 boundary; AI blocked from crossing.
- **O:** Two horizontal zones separated by a central vertical gate; arrows flow L-to-R from AI zone toward the gate; a perpendicular blockage bar stops AI from entering the human zone; human-zone nodes stand on the right of the gate.

## Figure 5 — The wrong arrow vs. the right arrow (career model)  ·  Important

**Figure type:** Two-panel before/after comparison schematic (two contrasting horizontal arrow paths).

**Block 1 — Illustrae paste block:** Create a flat vector comparison schematic on a white single-column canvas, 89mm wide, 300 DPI, with two stacked horizontal panels. The top panel shows the wrong arrow: a node representing the domain expert with an arrow leading rightward AWAY into a different node representing a generic technologist, with the original domain node fading or dropped — drawn so the domain identity is lost. Add a blockage symbol at the destination to mark that the market does not pay this path. The bottom panel shows the right arrow: the same domain-expert node retained on the left, with an additive arrow leading rightward to a combined node where the domain identity is preserved and an AI-capability element is layered on top — the domain node stays solid and intact. Map the wrong-arrow path and its dropped-domain outcome to vermillion, and the right-arrow path with its preserved domain plus added AI capability to bluish green; the domain-expert source node is sky blue in both panels. Use single-headed arrows only. Request an unannotated vector with no text.
