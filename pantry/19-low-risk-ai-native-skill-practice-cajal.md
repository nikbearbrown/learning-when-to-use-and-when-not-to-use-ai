## Figure 2 — The four-clause practice engine: AI administers, learner performs  ·  Important
**Heuristic:** MC
**Concept (one sentence):** Skill practice works when it is retrieval-based, immediately corrected, distributed, and at the learner's edge — and at every clause the learner's action (spell, self-correct, retrieve again, operate at own edge) is irreducible while AI only administers.
**Figure type:** Four-stage practice-loop diagram with a per-clause AI/learner split.

**Block 1 — Illustrae paste block:** Create a four-stage loop diagram on a white single-column canvas, 89mm wide, 300 DPI, flat vector. Show four clauses as stages connected by single-headed arrows in a practice loop: retrieval-based, immediately corrected, distributed, at-the-edge. At each stage, place two short side-by-side labels: what AI administers (generates the item, schedules the return, tracks mastery, adjusts difficulty) and what the learner performs (spells from sound, self-corrects, retrieves again, operates at own edge). The payload is that the learner action is irreducible at every stage — make the learner-performs side visually dominant throughout. Use bluish green for the learner-performs elements (the irreducible action, dominant), orange for the AI-administers elements, blue for the four clause nodes themselves, light gray for the loop arrows. Maximum eight labeled components. One-pixel strokes, white background, request an unannotated vector with no baked-in text.

**Block 2 — Full SCOPE prompt:**

Learner action irreducible at each stage.
